# Venture Dossier — CrowdMadness (Rethought)

> A massively-scalable **prediction-market game** — the 2026 reboot of MobThought (2004–08) — plus **CrowdResearch**, the B2B market-research line the game's forecast data feeds. A KingStrat portfolio company (CrowdMadness, Inc. — active-vs-reserved entity status TBD). The decade-arc centerpiece of the whole KSVGPS model, and the venture whose feasibility case *is* the regulatory radar (P-14).

## 1. The lineage, and why it matters structurally

MobThought (2004–08) built a prediction-market *game* with heavy this-is-not-gambling fine print, because real-money markets were illegal; the team ran it as a "background parallel thread," expecting US law to evolve. It stopped in 2008 over **prize-value risk** — even non-cash prizes (big-screen TVs for *Big Brother* forecasts) could read as constructive gambling to state AGs. A 2010–11 reboot was deferred for AdEvolve's sake. The law did evolve (2018 PASPA repeal → FanDuel/DraftKings → Polymarket/Kalshi/CFTC event contracts) — and no system flagged the shelved idea's feasibility change. **That miss is KSVGPS's founding lesson** ([`../00`](../00-PORTFOLIO-THESIS.md) §6), and MobThought→CrowdMadness is the canonical `successor-of` decade-arc: two ventures sharing Layer-A Ideas, never moved assets. The advisor bench (Bill Brand/HSN, Stephen McHenry/Google, Jonathan Forster/Spotify) imports as time-bounded People edges with an open re-engagement question.

**Why now (the economics inversion):** per-state + federal regulatory complexity that would once have cost millions in counsel is now a nightly LLM agent tracking every change and translating it into required app diffs. The radar is not ops tooling — **it is the product's load-bearing organ**, and (proposal) eventually a sellable RegTech line (S-05).

## 2. The three Ideas (never phases)

Per the canon test, three distinct Ideas: **game-money market** (CrowdMadness's channel; active/near); **real-money CFTC market** (serious-someday; brand undecided — the irreverent game tone is wrong for regulated trading; question-mark between a new brand and Patternicity; not-John founder-fit); **white-label platform** (defensive-someday; `patternicity.bet` held; upgraded by S-04 — dogfood the embedded-market card on PatternicityNews before pitching any external brand). CrowdResearch (SME survey portal + aggregated crowd-forecast data; `crowdresearch.com` held since 2008) is the B2B fourth piece, structure TBD (brand/subsidiary/division; one-funnel-vs-two).

## 3. Competitive posture

Meta's "Arena" prediction app (announced 2026-06-23) confirms the category and crowds the name-space (the Build-Line rename to `PredictionBracketsEngine` is recorded); Polymarket/Kalshi own real-money trading gravitas, Manifold owns play-money community. The open wedge is **the game**: bracket/tournament shapes ("Madness"), leagues, seasons, streaks, reputation — *fun first, market mechanics underneath* — plus the two data assets none of them build deliberately: calibrated forecaster reputation and sellable aggregate foresight (CrowdResearch). Gamified mechanics remain TBD/placeholder by John's explicit note; the brackets motif is the working center.

## 4. Ideation & Exploration

**Existing (carried):** the regulatory-tracking agent as first-class feature · market-pipeline cards with agent-suggested create/resolve next-actions (the GTD-reviewer pattern on market ops) · the self-refreshing **market dossier** LiveDocument per question (the living evidence behind the price) · CrowdResearch's self-serve survey portal · the game-as-data-engine inference (the crowd generates what CrowdResearch sells).

**Proposed (new this rethink):**
- **Auditable resolution (S-04):** markets settle against the Patternicity world graph with WeighTheFacts evidence chains, sealed as P-11 receipts — attacking the category's chronic resolution-dispute weakness with provenance instead of committee.
- **Question sourcing from the graph:** market-worthy questions generated from world-graph events, scored for resolvability before listing.
- **Embedded market cards** (`DiviaCard::CrowdMadness::market`) in PatternicityNews articles — distribution without user acquisition; the white-label demo built on owned property.
- **Forecast data as a World-Knowledge layer:** crowd priors flowing back into Patternicity's B2B feeds — the two ventures' data products compounding each other.
- **Internal milestone markets for KSVGPS (S-13):** the engine's first deployment — firm-internal, prize-less, zero regulatory surface, generating calibration data and dogfood polish before any public launch.
- **Reputation as the prize (launch posture):** leaderboards, seasons, calibration badges, and verifiable forecaster credentials (P-11) — status economies are legally clean and, for the target community, often stickier than merchandise.
- **RegTech spin-out option (S-05):** the jurisdiction-rule graph + compliance-diff engine productized for other regulated startups once proven here — a dated far target, not-John founder-fit.

**Rejected / Flawed:**
- **⛔ "It's a game, so prizes are safe" (R-05).** The exact 2008 stop-cause, and sweepstakes-model enforcement has *tightened* since. Repair: launch reputation-only; graduate prize types per-state strictly behind the radar's green lights; prize-value is a first-class jurisdiction variable in the compliance graph, and R-002 explicitly covers prize-value games, not just real money.
- **⛔ Treating real-money as the game's v2.** Different customer, different regulatory regime, different brand register — a separate Idea with its own venture-instantiation decision (the canon's own test); conflating them re-creates the v1/v2 collapse the whole model exists to prevent.
- **⛔ Launching the public game before the radar runs.** Sequencing error given the founding lesson: the compliance organ must be operational *first* (it is also the cheapest piece to build and the internal-markets deployment needs none of it — so the order is internal markets → radar → public game).
